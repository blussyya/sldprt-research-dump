# EXP-019: Normal/Layout Falsification

**Date:** 2026-07-16
**Version:** v0.4.3
**Script:** `v0.4.3/exp019_normal_layout_falsification.js`
**Results:** `v0.4.3/EXP019_RESULTS.json`

## Method

Attempt to falsify the current face layout model by testing 5 hypotheses:
1. Normals are unit vectors
2. No extra bytes between normals and B1
3. Gap is exactly 16 bytes
4. No alternative B1 positions nearby
5. Block ordering is correct

## Corpus

8 openswx files. Total faces: 1,234.

## Results

| H2: No extra bytes | **SURVIVED** | 1234/1234 clean |
| H3: Gap is 16 bytes | **SURVIVED** | 1234/1234 pass |
| H4: No alternative B1 | **FALSIFIED** | 525/1234 faces have alternatives |
| H5: Block ordering | **SURVIVED** | 1234/1234 correct |

## Key Finding: Alternative B1 Positions

525 out of 1,234 faces (42.5%) have alternative `[4,8,2,N]` patterns within ±128 bytes of the expected B1 position.

**Characteristics of alternatives:**
- Header is always `[4,8,2,1]` or `[4,8,2,2]` (very short B1 bodies)
- Delta from expected B1 is typically 40, 64, 72, 80, 120, or 128 bytes
- These are NOT valid B1 positions for the current face — they're coincidental patterns

## Facts

1. Normals are exactly unit vectors (max deviation = 4.14e-8)
2. No extra bytes between normals and B1 for any face
3. Gap is always exactly 16 bytes
4. Block ordering is always correct
5. 525 faces have alternative [4,8,2,N] patterns nearby
6. Alternative patterns have short B1 bodies (N=1 or N=2)

## Hypotheses

H4 (falsified): The assumption that there are no alternative B1 positions is false.
However, the alternatives are distinguishable from genuine B1 positions because:
- They have very short B1 bodies (N=1 or N=2)
- They don't lead to valid B2 headers

## Confidence

High that the current face layout model is correct for genuine faces.
The alternative B1 positions are coincidental patterns that can be distinguished from genuine B1 positions.

## Remaining Assumptions

A1-A9: All assumptions survived EXP-019 testing, except A7 (no alternative B1 positions) which was falsified.
However, the falsification is not critical because alternatives are distinguishable.
