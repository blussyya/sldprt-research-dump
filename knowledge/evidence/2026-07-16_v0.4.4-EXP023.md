## Observations

1. 661/1,172 faces (56.4%) have alternative [4,8,2,N] headers
2. N=1 accounts for 55.7% of alternatives, N=2 for 44.3%
3. VC=4 faces ALWAYS have alternatives (100% rate)
4. VC=8 faces ALWAYS have alternatives (100% rate)
5. VC=10 faces ALWAYS have alternatives (100% rate)
6. PTC has highest alternative rate (92.9%)
7. POCKET has lowest alternative rate (31.3%)
8. EC=4 faces always have N=1 alternatives
9. EC=5 faces have mix of N=1 and N=2

## Hypotheses

H1: The alternative header is a face property that depends on vertexCount (confidence: Medium)
H2: N=1 is used for faces with edgeCount=4 (confidence: Medium)
H3: N=2 is used for faces with edgeCount=5 (confidence: Low)
H4: The alternative header is optional and may be omitted in some files (confidence: Medium)
H5: PTC files use a different serialization strategy (confidence: Low)

## Confidence

High: Alternative headers are common (56.4% of faces)
High: VC=4,8,10 faces always have alternatives
Medium: N=1 correlates with EC=4
Low: N=2 correlates with EC=5; why some files have fewer alternatives

## Unknowns

1. Why do some faces have alternatives and others do not?
2. What determines N=1 vs N=2?
3. Why does POCKET have only 31.3% alternative rate?
4. Is the alternative header required or optional?
5. How does the alternative header affect parsing?
